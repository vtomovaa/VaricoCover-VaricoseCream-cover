WHAT ARE VARICOSE VEINS

Varicose veins are swollen and enlarged veins – usually blue or dark purple – that usually occur on the legs. They look lumpy, bulging or twisted in appearance and are caused by increased blood pressure in the veins. Varicose veins happen in the veins near the surface of the skin (superficial). The blood moves towards the heart by one-way valves in the veins. When the valves become weakened or damaged, blood can collect in the veins.
